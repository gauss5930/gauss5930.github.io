Abstract

We propose $\mathbf{K^{2} Eval}$, a novel benchamrk featuring 90 handwritten instructions that require in-depth knowledge of Korean language and culture for accurate completion.

The evaluation result of $\mathbf{K^{2} Eval}$ shows that while larger multilingual models with extensive training budgets may excel in traditional benchmarks featuring multiple-choice question formats, humans tend to prefer models with targeted training in the Korean language.

We also explore whether proprietary language models, such as GPT-4, can serve as substitutes for human evaluation. Our findings indicate that althoug scores derived from GPT-4 correlate with human evaluation, they fail to replicate the rankings determined by humans accurately.

$\mathbf{K^{2} Eval}$

Recent introductions of MT-Bench and Vicuna-Instructions-80 have brought forward compact yet high-quality collections as evaluation tools. Inspired by them, we create \mathbf{K^{2} Eval}, a collection of 90 handwritten Korean Instructions.

The design concept behind \mathbf{K^{2} Eval} centers on collecting instructions that necessitate knowledge specifci to Korean culture and context in order to solve. To ensure the diversity of our dataset, we have categorized Korean knowledge into nine distinct types and defined seven reasoning capabilities.
